Art Direction Update · V4
All characters and world art now render as 8-bit pixel art. Photorealistic AI characters and cinematic 3D worlds are everywhere — they signal generic AI startup. 8-bit grounds TETRA in the arcade cabinet aesthetic that Mickey already established. The math is sophisticated. The art is humble. That contrast is the brand.
Character 01 · The Grid · Primary
THE ARCHITECT
"The person who built what's inside the machine.
Not the machine."
Origin · Who They Are
The Story
The Architect has been inside the grid longer than anyone. Not because they were assigned there — because they built it, layer by layer, proof by proof. They discovered that the pointer-and-index paradigm was a wrong turn taken 50 years ago, and instead of complaining about it, they went back to the math and started over.

They are the embodiment of Mickey's origin story: the question "where do LLMs hide their data?" led somewhere nobody expected. The Architect followed that thread until they found the answer. Then they built a database around it.
The Philosophy
Simplicity is not the absence of complexity. It is the result of understanding it completely. The Architect doesn't simplify by removing — they simplify by going deeper until only the essential remains.

They believe the graph is the natural state of all data. Tables are a compression artifact — a lossy encoding of something richer. Every JOIN in the codebase is a monument to a decision that shouldn't have been made.

They are not cynical about this. They are hopeful. The math was always there. It just needed someone to find it.

Character 02 · The Map · Secondary
THE CARTOGRAPHER
"The one who can see the shape of everything
from a single vantage point."
Origin · Who They Are
The Story
The Cartographer didn't set out to map human civilization. They set out to understand why certain ideas survived and others didn't — why fire enabled metallurgy, why metallurgy enabled printing, why printing enabled science. They kept tracing the edges backward until they realized:

Everything is already a graph. It was always a graph. We just couldn't see the shape of it until now.

TETRA gave them the instrument they needed. With Retina's spectral layout, the eigenvectors resolve the graph into space — and the shape of human progress becomes visible for the first time.
The Philosophy
Every node is connected to everything else by a path. The length of that path is the measure of how alone any idea really is.

The Cartographer believes that the most important discoveries in history were not isolated breakthroughs — they were inevitable convergences, the moment when enough connected nodes finally reached a new vertex.

They see TETRA the same way: not as a product launch, but as the newest node on the graph of everything that got us here. It was always going to exist. Someone just had to follow the math far enough to find it.

The Architect answers how. The Cartographer answers why it matters. Together they address both audiences: the developer who wants the benchmark numbers, and the visionary who wants to understand what kind of world TETRA makes possible. They never appear together — they exist in different registers of the same world.
THE ARCADE CABINET AS SHARED STAGE
The arcade cabinet is the threshold between their worlds. The Architect lives inside the screen — they built the grid, the graph, the math. When you insert a coin and the demo begins, you're entering their domain. They are already there, calm, waiting, not performing.

The Cartographer stands outside, at the edge of the cabinet, looking at what the screen reveals. They see the civilization graph materialize and they name what it means — not technically, but historically. Cosmically. They are the person in the arcade who leans over your shoulder and says "do you realize what you're looking at?"

The player — the user, the developer, the prospect — is always in between. The Architect shows them the engine. The Cartographer shows them the stakes. TETRA is what makes both visions real.
