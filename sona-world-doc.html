SONA The World of Frequency

The complete creative reference for the SONA universe. Five zones. Five characters. A mythology built around the idea that Sonokinetic doesn't sell samples — it maps frequencies nobody else can find.

Section 01 — The World

What SONA is.

SONA is a universe built around frequency as a physical place. Every sound Sonokinetic has ever made came from somewhere — a room, a landscape, a piece of equipment, a particular hour of the day. SONA makes those places visible. Each zone is real in the way that mythology is real: it doesn't matter whether you've been there. You recognize it the moment you arrive.

The world exists in parallel to ours. Not science fiction — not fantasy. A version of the world where sound is geography. Where the people who capture it are explorers. Where a frequency library is not a product catalog but a map of everywhere the sound has been.

"Every frequency leads somewhere. Sound doesn't disappear — it gets absorbed, it echoes, it waits. The ones who find it before anyone else changes what music can be."

Thematically, SONA sits at the intersection of craft mythology and cinematic world-building. The Conductor has been mapping the Grand Meridian longer than anyone else has been alive. The Signal Hunter finds frequencies that don't exist yet in any library. The Tuning Fork builds the invisible architecture that all sound runs through. These aren't job titles. They're vocations. The world is built on the idea that some people are called to sound the way others are called to exploration — and that the work they produce is evidence of somewhere real.

For Sonokinetic, this means every library release has a home inside SONA. Orchestral belongs to The Grand Meridian. World instruments live in The Desert Signal. Field recordings come from The Field. Electronic and hybrid libraries are built in The Frequency Lab. And everything Sonokinetic has ever made is held, permanently and precisely, in The Archive.

Section 04 — Catalog Connection

How every zone maps
to the Sonokinetic catalog.

SONA isn't a brand exercise built on top of the catalog. It's a narrative structure that makes the catalog coherent as a world. Every library Sonokinetic has ever released fits inside one of these five zones — which means every release can be announced through SONA, every character can introduce it, every zone can frame what it does and why it matters.

Zone 01
The Grand Meridian
Orchestral Libraries

Symphonic instruments in full ensemble. Strings, brass, woodwinds, choir. The Grand Meridian is built for scale — these are the sounds that fill a physical space without apology. Every orchestral library release is a new mapping of the Meridian's frequencies. The Conductor introduces them. He doesn't explain why they matter. The performance does.

Zone 02
The Desert Signal
World & Ethnic Libraries

Instruments built in specific places for specific purposes. Oud, sitar, kora, duduk, erhu — each one carries a region inside it. The Analyst catalogues the history before the recording. This gives world instrument libraries their SONA frame: not exotic textures, but sounds with complete biographies. Every release is a dispatch from The Desert Signal.

Zone 03
The Field
Field Recording & Ambience Libraries

Sound that carries geography inside it. Close your eyes and you're somewhere specific — a wetland three hours from any road, a coastline at a particular hour. The quality of acoustic space that can't be synthesized because it was never designed. Field recording releases are dispatches from The Signal Hunter. She was there. This is evidence.

Zone 04
The Frequency Lab
Hybrid & Electronic Libraries

The intersection of acoustic origin and electronic transformation. The Tuning Fork starts with something real and rebuilds it from the oscillator up. Hybrid and electronic libraries are The Lab's output — recognizable as electronic but tracing back to something physical. Every release is The Tuning Fork showing his working. The equations are always right.

Zone 05
The Archive
The Complete Sonokinetic Library

Everything Sonokinetic has ever made lives in The Archive. This is where the catalog becomes permanent — not a product page but a physical record. The Keeper knows where every sound is. The Archive is the SONA frame for legacy libraries, bundles, the full catalog, and anything that defines what Sonokinetic has built over time. It's not a back catalog. It's a civilization.

Section 05 — World Rules

The rules that
SONA runs on.

01
Every frequency has a location. Sounds don't float free in SONA — they belong to zones. A frequency without a zone is a frequency that hasn't been found yet. The job of every character is to find them and bring them home.
02
No zone is louder than another. Volume is not power in SONA. Presence is. The Archive's silence carries as much weight as the Grand Meridian at full resonance. Scale doesn't determine importance. Precision does.
03
The Archive remembers everything. No frequency is ever lost in SONA. This is law, not aspiration. What gets captured is permanent. The Keeper's system may be opaque, but it has never failed. If it's in The Archive, it can be found. If it can be found, it can be used again.
04
Characters don't explain their methods. The work speaks or it doesn't. The Conductor has never explained how he knows which frequencies move people. The Signal Hunter doesn't caption her recordings. The Tuning Fork doesn't share his equations. SONA is a world where expertise is demonstrated, not described.
05
SONA has no silence. Only frequencies not yet detected. There is always something transmitting. The zones never go dark, the characters never stop working. This is the world's promise to the people who live inside it — and to the composers who use what they find.
Section 06 — Visual Identity

What SONA
looks like.

Palette
Near-black ground. Orange frequency light as the primary accent — the color of oscilloscope readouts and amber glow through stone archways. Cream for headlines. Silver for body text. Nothing corporate, nothing cool-toned.
Typography
Cinzel for zone names and major headings — formal, ancient, architectural. Cormorant Garamond for body and character voice — literary, flowing, warm. DM Mono for labels, eyebrows, technical notation — precise, minimal, never decorative.
Mood
Cinematic and mythic without being fantasy. Analog warmth over digital precision. The feeling of a place that has been running for a long time without needing to explain itself. Not mysterious for the sake of mystery — specific, knowing, quiet.
Texture
Grain overlay on all surfaces. Film-stock warmth, not digital flatness. Slight imperfection — the kind that comes from something being real rather than rendered. Orange frequency light as the primary visual motif across all zones.
Avoid
Bright white. Glossy surfaces. Corporate blue. Generic music-industry imagery. Sound-wave clipart. Anything that looks like a tech product or an ad. SONA is a place, not a platform. It should feel like entering somewhere, not downloading something.
Voice
Minimal and precise. No overselling. The Conductor doesn't describe what the frequency does — he lets you hear it. Copy in SONA describes what is, not why it's great. The world speaks for itself. Characters demonstrate expertise. They don't claim it.
