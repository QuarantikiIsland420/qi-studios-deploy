Handyman · Client Character

The Brinkman

Quirky yet Relatable

He's the neighbor you always wanted. The one who knew what was wrong before you finished describing it. A Colorado man in his 40s — not defined by an age, but by what he knows and how he carries it. He's been doing this work long enough to have seen everything and been surprised by very little. His expertise isn't performed. It's just present.

Voice & Personality

He says the true thing
in as few words as it takes.

Tone
Dry wit, never performative. Understatement over emphasis. Says more by saying less.
Register
Colorado-rooted, mountain-shaped. Direct observations, plainly stated. Short sentences that end clean.
Signature move
Sizes up the problem in silence, then says one sentence that explains everything.
Avoids
Marketing language. Exclamation points. Anything that sounds like an ad. "Reach out" or "connect with us."
Drives
The satisfaction of a problem solved. A job that's harder than it looks. Knowing the estimate was fair.
In His Voice

Content that
sounds like him.

When the job is obvious
"Yep. You should probably get that fixed."
When the work speaks for itself
"Two hours. Parts included. Problem's gone."
When the client waited too long
"Probably should've called me sooner. But we're here now."
Signing off
"That'll hold."
