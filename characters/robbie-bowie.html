Higher Learning

An intellectual wanderer who carries a suitcase full of ideas and a coat too warm for the tropics.

The Curriculum

He doesn't teach subjects.
He teaches how to think.

Robbie arrived on the island with more questions than answers and found that was the right way round.

Every lesson is short. Every idea is portable. He teaches from coastlines, not classrooms — because the best thinking happens when you're slightly uncomfortable.

The ones who change things rarely look like they're about to change anything.

I

The world rewards specific knowledge

On why knowing one thing deeply matters more than knowing everything shallowly.

II

Stories are how ideas travel

A lesson on narrative as the oldest delivery mechanism for meaning.

III

Curiosity is the only syllabus

Why the best education starts with a question, not an answer.

IV

Discomfort is just unfamiliarity

On how every new idea feels wrong before it feels obvious.

In His Own Words

"Most people are waiting to feel ready. Robbie has been ready since before he knew what he was ready for."

QI Studios — Character Notes, 2025

What Makes Him Work

Relatability

He's a little lost, on purpose

Audiences don't trust characters who have it all figured out. Robbie is searching — and that makes every idea he shares feel earned.

Authority

Curiosity as credential

He doesn't cite papers. He asks better questions. His authority comes not from expertise but from the quality of his attention.

Longevity

Infinite content, one character

Every idea is a new lesson. Every walk on the coast is a new episode. Robbie's world generates content naturally — it never runs dry.
